The philosophy

Most fitness apps want you to
be an athlete.
We just want you to move.

There's a 15-minute walk that counts. A backyard cricket game with the kids that counts. A swim at lunch that counts. A rest day you logged honestly — that counts too.

Activity doesn't care what it looks like. It only cares that you showed up. And the only way to know if you're showing up is to have somewhere honest to look.

get active. isn't trying to be your coach, your therapist, or your accountability partner. It's a mirror. A simple, honest daily record of whether you're moving through the world the way you want to.

No algorithm is telling you what to do. No leaderboard comparing you to strangers. Just your numbers, your goal, and the quiet satisfaction of a streak that's yours.

What it does

Everything you need.
Nothing you don't.

01
◎
Activity logging

Tap what you did. Add a note if you want. That's it. From a walk around the block to a 5am gym session — it all counts the same.

02
⌇
Body composition

Track weight, fat mass and lean mass. Seven-day rolling averages smooth the noise so you see the real trend, not yesterday's lunch.

03
◈
Goal tracking

Set your own activity target — 70%, 75%, 80% of days. A single bar shows exactly where you are and where you need to be right now.

04
◐
Diet quality

Rate your eating on a simple 1–5 scale. Not what you ate — just how well. Over time the pattern becomes very clear.

05
◇
Honest history

Every day is shown — active, rest, or unlogged. No hiding the gaps. The full picture is the only picture worth looking at.

06
◉
Your data, always

Export everything as a spreadsheet any time. No lock-in. No subscriptions. Your health data belongs to you — full stop.

How it works

A two-minute setup.
A twenty-second daily habit.

1
Set your goal

Choose what percentage of days you want to be active. Pick your activities. Set your year start date. Done in two minutes.

2
Log the day

Each evening — tap what you did, enter your weight, rate how you ate. Twenty seconds. Add it to your home screen so it's always one tap away.

3
Watch the picture form

After a week, the trends start to emerge. After a month, you have real data. After a year, you have an honest record of the life you lived.

The only rule
  • A 15-minute walk counts.
  • A swim at lunch counts.
  • Backyard cricket with the kids counts.
  • A rest day you logged honestly counts.
  • Showing up — in any form — always counts.

The only workout that doesn't count is the one you didn't log.
